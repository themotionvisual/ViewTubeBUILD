# Proposed Chart Collection Expansion

Based on the Gemini analytics insights, here are 10 high-priority charts to integrate into the Research Lab. These have been selected for their **unique visual styles** (styles we don't currently use) and their **algorithmic depth**.

## 1. The Geography of High CPMs
*   **Style**: **Interactive GeoChart / Heatmap**
*   **Why**: We currently have a bubble grid for geography, but a true world heatmap (using Google's `GeoChart`) is more professional for identifying localized revenue opportunities.
*   **Priority**: High (New Visual Style)

## 2. Published Time vs. Momentum
*   **Style**: **2D Grid Heatmap**
*   **Why**: A "time-block" heatmap (Day of Week vs. Hour of Day) is a staple for professional creators to optimize upload schedules. This introduces a grid-based visualization.
*   **Priority**: High (New Visual Style)

## 3. End Screen Effectiveness
*   **Style**: **Funnel Chart**
*   **Why**: Visualizes the "drop-off" of a viewer's journey. It moves from Total Views -> Elements Shown -> Clicks.
*   **Priority**: High (New Visual Style)

## 4. Series vs. Standalone Performance
*   **Style**: **Box Plot (Candlestick-like)**
*   **Why**: Shows the median, quartiles, and outliers. This helps a creator understand if their success is consistent or driven by a few "lucky" viral hits.
*   **Priority**: Medium (Complex Statistical Detail)

## 5. Seasonal Performance Radar
*   **Style**: **Radar (Spider) Chart**
*   **Why**: A circular view of the year (12 months) showing how RPM and Views stretch in different seasons. It provides a "signature" shape for the channel's annual cycle.
*   **Priority**: Medium (New Visual Style)

## 6. Thumbnail A/B Test Annotation
*   **Style**: **Annotated Line Graph**
*   **Why**: Not just a chart, but a tool. We add vertical "event" markers on a standard Views/CTR line chart to show exactly when a title or thumbnail was changed.
*   **Priority**: High (Innovative Tooling)

## 7. The "Algorithm Trigger" Matrix
*   **Style**: **Dual-Axis Multi-Line Graph**
*   **Why**: Overlays Impressions, CTR, and Views on a single timeline with two Y-axes. This visually captures the moment the "algorithm" takes over (Impressions spike as CTR settles).
*   **Priority**: Medium (Advanced Logic)

## 8. Operating System Revenue vs. Volume
*   **Style**: **Nested Donut (Sunburst Lite)**
*   **Why**: An inner ring for Volume (Views) and an outer ring for Revenue. This quickly highlights if a small demographic (e.g., iOS) is punchy enough to produce the majority of profit.
*   **Priority**: Medium (Refined Pie Style)

## 9. Long-Tail Shelf Life
*   **Style**: **Normalized Cohort Line Graph**
*   **Why**: Every video starts at "Day 0" on the X-axis. This lets you compare an "Evergreen" video (slow, steady slope) vs. a "Trend" video (vertical spike, then flatline) regardless of their actual publish date.
*   **Priority**: High (Powerful Strategic Insight)

## 10. The "Golden Ratio" Benchmark
*   **Style**: **Overlay Radar Chart**
*   **Why**: You select your most successful video as a "ghost" background on a radar chart, then compare new uploads against that shape to see which engagement metric (Likes, Shares, or AVD) is falling short.
*   **Priority**: High (Competitive Benchmarking)
